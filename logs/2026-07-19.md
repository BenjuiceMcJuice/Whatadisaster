## Pixel-art graphics overhaul ŌĆö fire maps + UI icons (branch `claude/app-graphics-animations-x72ur9`)

- Replaced the two growing-ellipse SVGs (`svgAshdown` / `svgCabot` + `fire*`/`emb*`/`cfire*`/`cemb*` shapes) with a single `<canvas id="fireCanvas">` (256├Ś141 internal, upscaled with `image-rendering:pixelated` ŌåÆ crisp chunky pixels at any size).

### Heatwave rework: wavy lines ŌåÆ site-severity impact board

Stakeholder feedback: the wavy red heat lines didn't work, and ŌĆö more fundamentally ŌĆö the heatwave infographic was weak because *heat doesn't spread like fire*. Reframed what escalates: not spatial spread, but **cumulative impact on vulnerable sites**, peaking at the tropical night (when temperature actually *falls* but risk peaks ŌĆö the key exercise lesson). A literal thermometer would mislead (stage-3 night reads "cooler"); so the gauge tracks heat-health *risk*, not temperature.

New heatwave rendering in `FMAP` (wildfire path untouched):
- Each vulnerable site (care home, Meridian/Northgate high-rises, school, hospital ED, Ashfield estate) gets a **status-coloured building outline** (green monitored ŌåÆ amber at risk ŌåÆ red critical) plus a **thermometer-style gauge** that rises and reddens. Building outline = at-a-glance status; gauge = the reading.
- **Escalating alert badge** (`LEVEL 4` + state) with a **time ┬Ę temp** readout: `15:00┬Ę40C` ŌåÆ `21:00┬Ę32C` ŌåÆ `02:00┬Ę28C`, so falling-temp / rising-risk is explicit.
- Severity keyed to stage index (`FMAP.render(... , idx)`): stage 1 mostly amber, stage 3 a board of red. **School de-escalates to green at night** (children home) ŌĆö realistic, and keeps the board readable rather than uniformly red.
- Critical (red) sites pulse; everything else static (same motion rule as wildfire). Reduced-motion shows a static frame with all rings lit.
- Simplified heat map labels (CARE HOME / SCHOOL / HOSPITAL) and dropped the stray 39C sun label (temp now lives in the badge).
