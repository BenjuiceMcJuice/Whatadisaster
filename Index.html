📋 Pre-Exercise Briefing

OPERATION ASHDOWN — What you need to know

What is this exercise?
This is a tabletop exercise — no real emergency. You'll be making decisions as a multi-agency responder. Your choices will be scored on how well they align with best practice emergency planning.

🌡️ Scenario Overview

Following 6 weeks of below-average rainfall and temperatures exceeding 32°C, a wildfire has ignited on Dundry Hill. Bristol's grasslands and woodland edges create significant fire spread risk. The M5 and A38 are at risk of closure. 8,000 residents in affected postcodes.

🏅 Command Structure

⬛ Gold — Strategic
Sets overall objectives, resources, policy. Usually off-scene.
🔘 Silver — Tactical
Translates Gold aims into action. Coordinates agencies at scene.
🔷 Bronze — Operational
Hands-on delivery. Manages specific tasks, sectors, resources.
🤝 JESIP
Joint Emergency Services Interoperability Principles — the rulebook.

🔵 JESIP Principles

CO-LOC
Co-locate — commanders from each agency meet at a Joint Emergency Services Control Point (JESCP)
COMMS
Communicate — use plain language, no jargon. Share information openly and early
COORD
Coordinate — agree shared objectives, use METHANE and M/ETHANE to pass situation reports
JDMP
Joint Decision Making — use the Joint Decision Model (JDM) to frame decisions together
SHARE
Shared Situational Awareness — everyone must have the same picture of what's happening

📞 Emergency Plan Escalation

L1
Business as Usual — internal agency response only
L2
Enhanced Response — multi-agency coordination, Bristol CEP notified
L3
Major Incident Declared — LRF activated, BCC Emergency Director on call
L4
Full Emergency — COBR consideration, national resource requests, community support hubs open
