🔥 Wildfire Behaviour

Spotting
Embers carried ahead of the fire front can land up to 2km away, starting spot fires. A wildfire can effectively jump roads via airborne embers — road clearance does not guarantee safety.
Urban WUI
Where wildland meets urban areas (WUI), fire behaviour is unpredictable. Garden fences, sheds, gas meters, and parked vehicles all become fuel. Fire can enter buildings through roof vents and gaps.
Wind effect
A 25mph wind can double or triple the rate of fire spread and dramatically increase ember cast distance. Wind direction shifts can cut off crews. Crew safety and dynamic risk assessment are paramount.
Slope
Fire travels uphill faster than downhill — up to 4x faster on steep slopes. Dundry Hill's north-facing slope means fire descending toward Ashfield has gravity working in its favour.
