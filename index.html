🔥 Wildfire Behaviour

Spotting
Embers carried ahead of the fire front can land up to 2km away, starting spot fires. A wildfire can effectively jump roads via airborne embers — road clearance does not guarantee safety.
Wildland Urban Interface
At the wildland urban interface (WUI), where wildland meets urban areas, fire behaviour is unpredictable. Garden fences, sheds, gas meters, and parked vehicles all become fuel. Fire can enter buildings through roof vents and gaps.
Wind effect
A 25mph wind can double or triple the rate of fire spread and dramatically increase ember cast distance. Wind direction shifts can cut off crews. Crew safety and dynamic risk assessment are paramount.
Slope
Fire travels uphill faster than downhill — up to 4x faster on steep slopes. Brynmoor Hill's north-facing slope means fire descending toward Ashfield has gravity working in its favour.

💼 Business Continuity Inject

During the exercise, consider: Your organisation's HQ building is within the affected zone and staff have been told to work remotely with 30 minutes' notice. Key personnel are unreachable. Critical systems are inaccessible.

Discussion prompt: How long could your organisation sustain core emergency functions? Who authorises alternative working arrangements? Is this covered in your Business Continuity Plan?

📂 Response Plan Check

Before starting, locate your organisation's Emergency Response Plan or Major Incident Plan. As you play, note whether your plan covers the situations you encounter and flag any gaps for the post-exercise discussion.
