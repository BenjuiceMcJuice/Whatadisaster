# Stakeholder Feedback — June 2026

Feedback collected from partner agencies following initial playtesting of the simulator.

---

## Clare (Emergency Planning)

**Overall:** Really impressed as a tool for testing knowledge and understanding, particularly good for helping people understand other agency roles and decision making.

### Issues

**Timed questions too short**
The countdowns are too quick. Suggested approach: show question first, then user clicks to reveal options and start the countdown. If that's not feasible, simply extend the countdown.

**Inaccurate answer options — TCG / hospital MI plan**
One option states TCG requests hospital to implement MI plan. This would be the hospital's own decision, not a TCG request.

**Inaccurate answer option — mutual aid in wildfire scenario**
Fire commander requesting mutual aid references NWFC. Should be NRFC (National Resilience Fire Control). Also flagged that this would be a Fire commander matter, not a TCG request.

**Crew safety in extreme heat**
Dynamic risk assessment should apply only if no other crew members are symptomatic.

**Ambulance surge management**
Surge management would be an internal matter for the Ambulance service to manage, not a TCG decision.

**Answer telegraphing**
After playing a few roles, the correct answer becomes easier to spot due to the increased level of detail in the correct option. Subtle but noticeable after multiple plays. Clare notes this may not actually be a problem — players are learning either way.

### Positives

- Strong potential for developing across a range of scenarios
- Interested in working alongside the team to develop further

## Jon Muddel (Utilities)

**Overall:** Good idea; lets people see what else is going on in incidents like these.

### Issues

**Utilities category too broad**
Energy and water operate very differently — grouping them as a single "utilities" role is an oversimplification, similar to putting all blue light responders together. Acknowledged this is a hard problem if splitting would result in very short sections.

**Water missing from extreme heat scenario**
Water has no representation in Operation Solstice (heatwave), but extreme heat creates serious operational difficulties for water supply. Jon offered to suggest a couple of examples to add in — worth following up.

**Countdowns too short**
Immediate-decision timers are a little short; people need more time to read the options before the countdown starts.
