## 2. Met Office / UKHSA Heat-Health Alert System

Incorporate the **Heat-Health Alert (HHA)** system into Operation Solstice questions, context text, and answer feedback.

**Alert levels (impact-based, introduced summer 2023 — run jointly by UKHSA and Met Office, June–Sept):**

| Level | Colour | Response | Who's affected |
|-------|--------|----------|----------------|
| 1 | 🟢 Green | Preparedness | Minimal impact — ensure plans are in place |
| 2 | 🟡 Yellow | Response | Mainly vulnerable populations (elderly, multiple health conditions, polypharmacy) |
| 3 | 🟠 Amber | Enhanced response | Whole health service + other sectors; coordinated response required; may be issued alongside NSWWS Extreme Heat warning |
| 4 | 🔴 Red | Emergency response | Risk to life for healthy population; always issued with red NSWWS Extreme Heat warning; all sectors; coordinated response essential |

**Other key facts:**
- Extraordinary alerts can be issued outside the June–Sept window, in which case the **Adverse Weather and Health Plan** applies
- Aligned with the Met Office **National Severe Weather Warning Service (NSWWS)**
- Outside the seasonal window, refer to the Adverse Weather and Health Plan

**Suggested decision points:**
- When to escalate from Yellow to Amber, and what that triggers
- What a Red alert requires across sectors (not just health)
- Distractors: confusing which level requires cross-sector vs health-only coordination

## 5. New Role: Voluntary Agency Coordinator — Both Scenarios

Add a **Voluntary Agency Coordinator** (or "Third Sector Liaison") role to both **Operation Solstice** and **Operation Cabot**.

**Guidance basis:** [GOV.UK — How voluntary organisations can help in emergencies](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/how-volunteers-can-help-in-emergencies)

**Key principles for decisions and feedback:**
- Voluntary organisations **support** Category 1 and 2 responders — they don't replace statutory services
- They operate through **Local Resilience Forums (LRFs) / Strategic Co-ordination Groups (SCGs)**
- The **Civil Contingencies Act 2004** requires Category 1 responders to "have regard" to voluntary sector activities in emergency planning
- The **Voluntary Sector Civil Protection Forum (VSCPF)** (Cabinet Office + British Red Cross) provides the engagement framework between government, emergency services, local authorities, and voluntary orgs
- Chapter 14 of *Emergency Preparedness* covers voluntary sector roles across planning, response, and recovery

**Suggested decision points to test:**
- At what point in the incident should voluntary agencies be activated/stood up?
- Who coordinates voluntary agencies — and what's the risk of unsolicited self-deployment?
- What tasks are appropriate for volunteers vs statutory responders? (e.g. welfare/rest centres/community liaison vs frontline response)
- How do voluntary agencies feed into the SCG structure without bypassing it?
