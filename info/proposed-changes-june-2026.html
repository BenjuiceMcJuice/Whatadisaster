1. Countdown Timers — Too Short

Raised by: Clare & Jon (independently)

Four questions currently use a countdown pressure timer. Current durations are:

QuestionRoleScenarioCurrent Timer
Power Grid StressUtilitiesHeatwave12 seconds
Crew Safety in Extreme HeatFireHeatwave10 seconds
Substation FailureUtilitiesHeatwave10 seconds
Heat Stroke RecognitionAmbulanceHeatwave12 seconds

Both reviewers found these too short — not enough time to read the options before the clock runs out.

Option A — Extend the timers (low effort): Increase all timed questions to 25–30 seconds. A quick code change.

Option B — Click-to-reveal (Clare's suggestion, medium effort): Show the question text first, then the player clicks a button to reveal the answer options and start the countdown. This replicates real decision pressure more authentically — you know the situation before you're forced to choose. This would be a small UX addition to the existing timer logic.

Recommendation: Option B is the better experience if we have time for it; Option A as a quick interim fix regardless.

2. Content Accuracy — Factual Corrections

Raised by: Clare

2a. TCG requesting hospital to implement MI plan

One answer option currently states that the TCG requests the hospital to activate their Major Incident Plan. Clare notes this is incorrect: activating an MI Plan is the hospital's own decision, not something the TCG directs.

Proposed fix: Rephrase the option so the TCG notifies or alerts the hospital and ensures they are aware of the situation — not that the TCG requests activation. The hospital makes its own decision based on that notification.

2b. Mutual aid in the wildfire scenario — NWFC vs NRFC

The wildfire scenario (Operation Ashdown) has a question where aerial mutual aid is requested via NWFC. Clare flagged two issues:

  • The correct abbreviation is NRFC (National Resilience Fire Control), not NWFC
  • This would be a Fire commander decision, not a TCG request
Proposed fix: Update the question text and relevant answer options to reference NRFC. Reframe the decision so it sits with the Fire commander rather than being presented as a TCG action.

2c. Crew safety in extreme heat — dynamic risk assessment

The correct answer for the "Crew Safety in Extreme Heat" question needs a small but important caveat: a dynamic risk assessment is the right response if no other crew members are symptomatic. Without that condition the answer implies continuing work regardless of wider crew welfare, which is not the correct doctrine.

Proposed fix: Add the conditional clause to the correct option so it reads something like: "Withdraw the affected crew member, dynamic risk assessment for remaining crew if no others are symptomatic, continue operation."

2d. Ambulance surge management

A Phase 3 question frames surge management as a decision to be made at the TCG level. Clare notes that surge management is an internal Ambulance service matter — SWAST manages it internally, not via TCG direction.

Proposed fix: Reframe the question so the player is acting as the Ambulance commander deciding how to manage internal surge, rather than a TCG decision. The TCG might be informed of the situation, but the management action sits with Ambulance.

3. Answer Telegraphing

Raised by: Clare

After playing multiple roles, Clare noticed that the correct answer tends to stand out because it contains more detail than the other options. This makes it easier to spot through pattern recognition rather than knowledge.

Clare's own view: "it means players are learning anyway, so maybe not really a problem at all."

Options:
  • Leave as-is — players are learning the right behaviours regardless of how they spot the answer. The detail in the correct option is itself educational.
  • Add detail to distractors — make the incorrect options more plausible and similarly detailed, so the right answer isn't telegraphed by length or specificity. This requires careful writing to avoid making wrong answers look right to non-experts.
Recommendation: Worth flagging to Heather for a view, but not a priority fix. The learning value holds either way.

4. Utilities Role Coverage

Raised by: Jon Muddel

4a. Single utilities role is too broad

Jon notes that energy and water utilities operate very differently and have different responsibilities, command structures, and concerns during emergencies. Grouping them as a single "Utilities" role is an oversimplification — analogous to combining all blue light services into one role.

Options:
  • Split into two roles: Energy and Water. Each would need its own question bank (~3 questions per scenario). Jon has offered to help provide content for water. High effort but significantly improves authenticity.
  • Keep single role, add water content: Retain the utilities role but ensure both energy and water perspectives are represented within it. Lower effort, partial improvement.
  • Rename and reframe: Keep one role but make it explicitly "Infrastructure Utilities" with a broader framing, and add a note in the briefing that it covers both energy and water considerations.
Recommendation: Option 1 (split) is the right long-term direction. Jon's offer of help makes it tractable. Could be scoped as a Phase 2 development.

4b. Water supply absent from heatwave scenario

The heatwave scenario (Operation Solstice) currently has no water supply content, despite extreme heat creating significant operational challenges for water networks: pressure drops, increased demand, pipe failures in heat, and supply management.

Jon has explicitly offered to provide example scenarios from real operational experience.

Proposed fix: Follow up with Jon to gather 1–2 real-world examples for the heatwave scenario. These could be added to the existing utilities question bank in the short term, or form the basis of a dedicated water role if we split utilities.
